Kleanthis Malialis

I am a Research Associate with the KIOS Research and Innovation Center of Excellence at the University of Cyprus, working with Prof. Marios Polycarpou and Prof. Christos Panayiotou. My current research interests lie within the area of learning from nonstationary, limited-labelled, and imbalanced data streams.

Previously, I was a Marie Sk≈Çodowska-Curie Fellow at the University of Cyprus, a visiting researcher with the Polytechnic University of Milan (POLIMI) working with Prof. Manuel Roveri and Prof. Cesare Alippi, and a post-doctoral researcher with the Department of Computer Science at University College London (UCL) working with Prof. Jun Wang. I also held Data Scientist roles in industry (London).

I hold a PhD degree from the Department of Computer Science at the University of York, UK, with a focus on multiagent (reinforcement) learning and coordination for network intrusion (e.g., DDoS attacks) detection and response. It was supervised by Dr. Daniel Kudenko and funded by an EPSRC Doctoral Training Accounts (DTA) Studentship. Prior to my PhD studies, I obtained my MEng degree in Computer Systems and Software Engineering from the same department.
